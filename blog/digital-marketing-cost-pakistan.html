"How much does digital marketing cost in Pakistan?" is one of the most common questions business owners ask when they decide to invest in growing their online presence. The honest answer is: it depends. But that is not helpful on its own. In this guide, we break down real, current pricing for every major digital marketing service in Pakistan — from SEO and paid ads to social media management, web design, and influencer campaigns. Whether you are just starting out or looking to scale, this guide will help you understand what is a fair price, what is overpriced, and what is suspiciously cheap.

1. Overview: Why Digital Marketing Prices Vary So Much in Pakistan

If you have asked for quotes from a few agencies or freelancers, you have probably noticed massive variation in pricing — sometimes a 10x difference for what sounds like the same service. This happens for several reasons.

Experience and Track Record

A freelancer with one year of experience will charge far less than a seasoned agency with 5+ years and proven results. With marketing, you largely get what you pay for. A cheap SEO service that uses black-hat techniques might get your website penalized by Google, while a reputable agency's work compounds positively over time.

Scope of Services

Two agencies might both say they offer "social media management," but one creates 3 posts per month while another creates 20 posts, writes captions, runs ads, and responds to comments. Always clarify exactly what is included before comparing prices.

Location Within Pakistan

Agencies based in Karachi and Islamabad tend to charge more than those in smaller cities. However, geography matters less when working with remote-first agencies — the best agencies can serve clients nationwide.

2. SEO Services Cost in Pakistan

SEO is a long-term investment that builds organic traffic over time. The pricing depends on the competitiveness of your industry and the scope of work involved.

Service Level Monthly Cost (PKR) What's Included
Basic SEO PKR 15,000–30,000 On-page optimization, basic keyword targeting, monthly report
Standard SEO PKR 30,000–70,000 Full on-page + off-page, content creation, link building, technical audit
Advanced SEO PKR 70,000–150,000+ Comprehensive strategy, high-DA link building, competitor analysis, e-commerce SEO

One-Time SEO Audit

A professional one-time SEO audit typically costs PKR 10,000–40,000 depending on the size of your website and depth of the audit. This is a good starting point if you want to understand your current SEO situation before committing to monthly services.

What to Watch Out For

Be very cautious of any agency promising you "#1 on Google in 30 days" for very low prices. This almost always means they are using black-hat SEO techniques (buying spammy links, keyword stuffing) that will eventually result in a Google penalty and can destroy your website's search presence.

3. Social Media Management Cost in Pakistan

Social media management includes creating content, scheduling posts, engaging with followers, and sometimes reporting. Pricing varies based on the number of platforms and posts per month.

Plan Monthly Cost (PKR) Typically Includes
Starter (1 platform) PKR 20,000–40,000 8–12 posts/month, basic captions, 1 platform
Growth (2 platforms) PKR 40,000–80,000 15–20 posts/month, captions, Stories, basic engagement
Professional (3+ platforms) PKR 80,000–200,000+ Full management, Reels/video, community management, reporting

Note that these prices typically do not include the cost of running paid ads. Ad spend is usually billed separately and goes directly to Meta or Google, not to the agency.

4. Facebook and Instagram Ads Cost in Pakistan

For paid advertising, there are two types of costs: the agency/freelancer management fee and the actual ad spend that goes to Meta (Facebook/Instagram).

Agency Management Fee

Most Pakistani agencies charge either a flat monthly fee or a percentage of your ad spend for managing your campaigns:

  • Flat fee model: PKR 20,000–60,000/month for campaign setup and management
  • Percentage model: 15–25% of monthly ad spend (minimum fees usually apply)

Facebook Ad Spend (Budget for Pakistan)

How much you spend on the actual ads depends on your goals, but here are realistic minimum budgets for effective campaigns targeting Pakistan:

  • Awareness campaigns: PKR 5,000–15,000/month
  • Lead generation: PKR 15,000–50,000/month for meaningful lead volume
  • E-commerce / sales campaigns: PKR 30,000–100,000+/month for profitable ROAS

Cost Per Result

Average costs when targeting Pakistani audiences (these vary widely by industry and ad quality):

  • Cost Per Click (CPC): PKR 5–30 per click
  • Cost Per Lead (CPL): PKR 100–800+ per lead
  • Cost Per 1,000 Impressions (CPM): PKR 80–400

5. Content Marketing and Copywriting Cost in Pakistan

Content marketing includes blog writing, website copywriting, email newsletters, and video scripts. Quality content is the foundation of SEO and organic social growth.

Content Type Cost Per Unit (PKR) Notes
Blog post (800–1200 words) PKR 3,000–15,000 Price rises with research depth and expertise required
Website copywriting (per page) PKR 5,000–20,000 Home, About, Services pages require strong persuasive writing
Social media captions (monthly) PKR 8,000–25,000 Usually 15–20 captions per month
Email newsletter PKR 2,000–8,000 each Writing only; email platform costs are separate

6. Website Design and Development Cost in Pakistan

Your website is your digital headquarters — it needs to be fast, beautiful, and built to convert visitors into customers. Prices in Pakistan vary significantly based on complexity.

Website Type One-Time Cost (PKR) Timeline
Landing Page Only PKR 20,000–60,000 1–2 weeks
Business / Agency Website (5–10 pages) PKR 60,000–200,000 3–6 weeks
E-Commerce Store (basic) PKR 80,000–250,000 4–8 weeks
Custom E-Commerce / Web App PKR 200,000–1,000,000+ 2–6 months

Ongoing Maintenance

Most websites require monthly maintenance (hosting, security updates, content updates): budget PKR 5,000–20,000/month for ongoing website care.

7. Influencer Marketing Cost in Pakistan

Influencer marketing in Pakistan has grown dramatically, and it can be an extremely cost-effective way to reach new audiences. Rates are based on an influencer's follower count, engagement rate, and content quality.

Influencer Tier Followers Cost Per Post (PKR)
Nano Influencer 1K–10K PKR 2,000–10,000
Micro Influencer 10K–100K PKR 10,000–50,000
Mid-Tier Influencer 100K–500K PKR 50,000–200,000
Macro Influencer 500K–1M+ PKR 200,000–1,000,000+

Pro Tip: Micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) often deliver the best ROI in Pakistan. They have higher engagement rates and more authentic connections with their audiences compared to mega-influencers.

8. Full Digital Marketing Package Cost in Pakistan

Many agencies offer comprehensive monthly retainer packages that bundle multiple services. Here is what you can realistically expect to pay for a full digital marketing package:

  • Starter Package: PKR 40,000–80,000/month — Social media management (1–2 platforms) + basic SEO
  • Growth Package: PKR 80,000–150,000/month — Social media (3 platforms) + SEO + content marketing + ad management
  • Enterprise Package: PKR 150,000–400,000+/month — Full-service including ads management, influencer outreach, video content, PR, and dedicated account manager

These figures exclude the actual ad spend budget, which is an additional cost paid directly to platforms like Meta and Google.

9. Freelancer vs Agency: What You Get for Your Money

Hiring a Freelancer

Pakistani freelancers (found on Fiverr, Upwork, or locally) typically cost 40–70% less than agencies. However, a single freelancer can only do so many things well. They might excel at Facebook Ads but be weak on design or content writing. Freelancers also carry more risk — if they become busy or unavailable, your marketing stops.

Hiring an Agency

A full-service digital marketing agency brings a team of specialists — a copywriter, designer, ads specialist, SEO expert, and account manager — all working on your account. The work is more comprehensive, consistent, and backed by established processes. The trade-off is the higher monthly cost.

The Smart Approach

For most small businesses in Pakistan, the best starting point is to hire a specialist freelancer for one core service (e.g., Facebook Ads), prove ROI, and then invest those returns into a broader agency engagement when you are ready to scale.

10. How to Set Your Digital Marketing Budget in Pakistan

A common rule of thumb is to allocate 10–20% of your revenue to marketing. For a business generating PKR 500,000/month, that is PKR 50,000–100,000 for marketing — which is a reasonable budget to start seeing meaningful results with a combination of SEO and paid ads.

If you are just starting out and your revenue is low, prioritize the channels with the highest immediate ROI: Facebook and Instagram ads usually deliver faster results than SEO, but SEO builds lasting value. An ideal split might be 60% paid ads, 30% SEO, and 10% content creation in the early stages.

Conclusion

Digital marketing in Pakistan is highly accessible at almost every budget level. You do not need to spend millions to start seeing results. The key is to invest strategically — start with the service most likely to generate immediate revenue, measure results carefully, and reinvest your returns into expanding your marketing efforts over time.

When evaluating agencies or freelancers, do not just compare prices. Compare results. Ask for case studies, client references, and specific examples of results they have delivered for businesses similar to yours. The right marketing partner will save you money by delivering measurable ROI — not just activity.
