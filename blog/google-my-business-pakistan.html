How to Set Up Google My Business in Pakistan: Complete Guide (2026)

If your business isn't on Google Maps, you're invisible to local customers. Here's exactly how to set up, verify, and optimize your Google My Business profile in Pakistan.

If you run a local business in Pakistan — whether it's a restaurant in Karachi, a clinic in Lahore, a shop in Islamabad, or a service business in any city — Google My Business (GMB) is one of the most powerful free tools available to you. When someone searches "restaurants near me" or "best dentist in Lahore" on Google, GMB is what determines which businesses show up on that map. And in my experience, most Pakistani businesses either don't have a GMB profile at all, or they've set one up but never optimized it properly.

1. Why Google My Business Matters in Pakistan

Let me explain why this is such a big deal with a real example. A friend of mine runs a small dental clinic in Gulshan-e-Iqbal, Karachi. He had a nice clinic, good reviews from patients, but almost no new patients coming in from online searches. Why? Because when you searched "dentist Gulshan-e-Iqbal" on Google, his clinic didn't appear anywhere. He was completely invisible to people searching online.

After we set up and optimized his Google My Business profile, within two months, he was showing up in the top 3 results on Google Maps for multiple keywords. New patient inquiries went up by about 35% — all without spending a single rupee on ads.

Key Benefits for Pakistani Businesses

  • Free visibility on Google: Your business appears on Google Search and Google Maps when people search for your products or services locally.
  • Trust and credibility: A complete GMB profile with photos, reviews, and business details makes your business look professional and trustworthy.
  • Direct customer actions: People can call you, get directions, visit your website, or message you directly from your GMB listing — reducing friction between finding you and contacting you.
  • Free customer insights: GMB provides analytics showing how many people found your profile, what keywords they used, and what actions they took.
  • Mobile-first discovery: With most Pakistanis searching on mobile, Google Maps results appear prominently above traditional website results.

2. Step-by-Step: Setting Up Google My Business

Let me walk you through the exact process. It's straightforward, but there are some important details you shouldn't skip.

Step 1: Go to Google Business Profile

Visit business.google.com and sign in with your Google account. If you don't have one, create a Gmail account specifically for your business.

Step 2: Enter Your Business Name

Type your exact business name as it appears in the real world. Don't try to stuff keywords here — "Ahmed's Dental Clinic" is correct, "Best Dentist Karachi Ahmed's Dental Clinic" is wrong and Google may suspend your listing for it.

Step 3: Choose Your Business Category

Select the most accurate primary category. Google uses this to determine which searches your business appears for. For example, "Dentist" is better than just "Medical." You can add additional categories later. In my experience, choosing the right primary category is one of the biggest ranking factors for local SEO in Pakistan.

Step 4: Add Your Location

If you have a physical store or office where customers visit, add your exact address. Google will show your location on Google Maps. Be precise — use the correct street name, area, and city. For businesses without a physical location (like home-based services), you can choose to serve customers at their location and specify your service area instead.

Step 5: Add Contact Information

Add your phone number and website URL. For Pakistani businesses, make sure to use the phone format that local customers will call. If you don't have a website, that's okay — GMB still works without one, but having a website is always recommended. If you need help building one, check out our web development services.

Step 6: Verify Your Business

Google needs to verify that your business is real. For most Pakistani businesses, verification happens through one of these methods:

  • Postcard by mail: Google sends a physical postcard with a verification code to your business address. This typically takes 5–14 days in Pakistan (sometimes longer in smaller cities).
  • Phone verification: Available for some businesses — Google calls or texts a code to your registered number.
  • Email verification: Available for select business types.
  • Video verification: Google may ask you to record a short video of your business and surroundings.

Be patient with the verification process. Don't create multiple listings or try to "game" the system — this can get your profile suspended permanently.

3. Optimizing Your GMB Profile for Maximum Visibility

Setting up a profile is just the beginning. The businesses that actually rank at the top of Google Maps are the ones that have fully optimized their profiles. Here's what to do after setup:

Complete Every Single Field

Google rewards completeness. Fill in your business description (750 characters), hours of operation, services offered, and attributes (like "wheelchair accessible" or "Wi-Fi available"). The more complete your profile, the better Google understands your business and the higher it ranks.

Add High-Quality Photos

This is huge. Businesses with photos get 42% more requests for directions and 35% more clicks to their website compared to those without. Upload clear, well-lit photos of your storefront, interior, products, and team. Avoid stock photos — use real images of your actual business. I've seen Pakistani businesses completely ignore photos, and it's one of the easiest wins you can get.

Write a Compelling Business Description

Use your 750-character description wisely. Include your main services, location, and what makes you unique. Write naturally — don't keyword-stuff. For example: "Family dental clinic in Gulshan-e-Iqbal, Karachi, offering general dentistry, orthodontics, and cosmetic treatments. Serving patients across DHA and surrounding areas since 2015."

Set Accurate Business Hours

Keep your hours updated, including special hours for holidays like Eid, Independence Day, and Ramadan. There's nothing worse than a customer showing up to find your business closed when Google says you're open.

Add Your Products and Services

GMB lets you list individual products and services with descriptions and prices. Use this feature — it helps Google match your listing with specific searches and gives customers more reasons to choose you.

4. Getting More Google Reviews (The Right Way)

Reviews are one of the top ranking factors for Google Maps. More importantly, they build trust with potential customers. In my experience working with Pakistani businesses, most owners know reviews matter but don't actively ask for them. Here's a practical system:

Ask Every Happy Customer

The simplest strategy is also the most effective: ask satisfied customers to leave a review. Do it in person after a successful service, or send a follow-up WhatsApp message with your Google review link.

Make It Easy

Create a short review link using your GMB dashboard and share it via WhatsApp, SMS, or printed QR codes. If your customers have to search for your business on Google just to leave a review, most won't bother. One restaurant client of ours placed QR codes on table tents — their reviews went from 15 to 250+ in three months.

Respond to Every Review

Always respond to reviews — both positive and negative. Thank positive reviewers genuinely. For negative reviews, respond professionally, acknowledge the issue, and offer to resolve it offline. This shows potential customers that you care about feedback. Google also considers review responses as a positive signal.

Never Buy Fake Reviews

I've seen some Pakistani businesses try to shortcut this by buying fake reviews. Don't do it. Google is very good at detecting fake reviews and will remove them — sometimes suspending your entire listing in the process.

5. Using GMB Posts to Stay Active

Google My Business has a built-in posting feature that most businesses completely ignore. You can publish updates, offers, events, and products directly on your GMB profile. These posts appear in your Knowledge Panel when people search for your business.

Types of Posts You Can Create

  • What's New: Business updates, new arrivals, announcements
  • Offers: Discounts, promotions, Eid sales
  • Events: Upcoming events with dates and times
  • Products: Showcase individual products with photos and prices

Posting Strategy

Post at least once a week. GMB posts expire after 7 days, so consistency is key. Include a photo, a clear description, and a call-to-action (like "Call Now," "Visit Us," or "Order Online"). In the Pakistani market, I've found that posts about special offers and Eid promotions get the most engagement.

6. Common GMB Mistakes Pakistani Businesses Make

  • Using a fake or virtual address: Google will eventually catch this and suspend your listing. Only list addresses where customers can actually visit.
  • Keyword-stuffing the business name: Your business name should be your real registered name — not "Best Biryani Karachi Cheap Food Home Delivery."
  • Never updating the profile: A profile with outdated hours, no photos, and zero posts looks abandoned. Keep it fresh and active.
  • Ignoring reviews: Not responding to reviews (especially negative ones) is a missed opportunity. Engage with every review.
  • Creating duplicate listings: Having multiple GMB listings for the same location confuses Google and splits your reviews. Stick to one verified listing per location.
  • Not using the Insights tab: GMB provides valuable data about how people find you, what keywords they use, and what actions they take. Check this monthly and use the data to improve.

Conclusion

Google My Business is one of the highest-ROI marketing activities any local business in Pakistan can do — and it's completely free. The businesses that take the time to set up, optimize, and actively manage their GMB profiles consistently outperform competitors in local search results.

The key is to treat your GMB profile like a living asset, not a one-time setup. Post regularly, collect reviews, respond to customers, and keep your information accurate. If you do these things consistently, you'll start seeing results within weeks — not months.
